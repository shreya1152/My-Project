VISIT SATARA



About Satara

Satara is a city and district in the state of Maharashtra, India, known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and scenic landscapes. It was once the capital of the Maratha Empire and is historically significant as the seat of the Chhatrapati of the Maratha Empire, Shahu I. The city is also recognized for its seven hills, which is reflected in its name, meaning "seven hills" in Marathi.Satara served as the capital of the Maratha Empire under the rule of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Satara boasts several historical monuments and forts, reflecting its connection to Maratha history. Popular dishes include Bharli Vangi (stuffed eggplant) and Puran Poli (sweet flatbread)After the fall of the Maratha Confederacy, Satara became a rump state ruled by the Bhonsle dynasty, descendants of Shivaji, which was eventually annexed by the British using the Doctrine of Lapse.



Places to Visit Satara

Kas Pathar

Kaas has more than 850 different species of flowering plants including orchids and Drosera Indica.

Sajjangad Fort

Originally Parali Fort, it became the final abode of Samarth Ramdas, mentor of Shivaji Maharaj.

Thoseghar Waterfall

Best during monsoon, this powerful waterfall is a must-see natural beauty of Satara.

Venna Lake

Enjoy peaceful boat rides at Venna Lake, built by the Satara king in 1842.

Famous Food in satara

Kandi Pedha

Satara Kandi Pedha is a renowned sweet originating from Satara, Maharashtra, known for its unique flavor and soft, fudge-like texture. It is primarily made from milk, sugar, and cardamom. The traditional recipe involves slow-cooking khoya and sugar until thickened, with the addition of ghee and a blend of secret spices.

Puran poli

Puran poli is an Indian sweet flatbread that is popular in South India and the state of Maharashtra. It is also known as puran puri, holige, obbattu, bobbatlu, poley, bakshamulu, and boli.Puran Poli is a traditional Indian sweet flatbread.

Bharli Vangi

The Maharashtrian Bharli Vangi is a classic curry made by stuffing baby eggplants with a hot and sweet mixture of diced onion, freshly grated coconut, ground jaggery, crushed roasted peanuts, chopped cilantro/coriander leaves, a hint of heat from red chili powder, and the quintessential Marathi kala/goda masala.
