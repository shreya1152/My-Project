Places to Visit Satara

Kas pathar

Kaas has more than 850 different species of flowering plants. These include orchids, shrubs such as the Karvy, and carnivorous plants such as Drosera Indica. Kaas Pathar is a plateau made from volcanic rocks in the Satara district of Maharashtra, and comes under the biosphere of the Western Ghats.it became a part of a UNESCO World Natural Heritage Site in 2012.

Sajjangad Fort

Originally known as Parali Fort, it was later renamed Sajjangad, meaning “Fort of Good People,” after becoming the final abode of Samarth Ramdas, the revered spiritual mentor of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Today, it stands as both a revered pilgrimage site and a proud symbol of the Maratha legacy.

Thoseghar waterfall

The best time to experience Thoseghar Waterfall is during the monsoon season, from July to November. During this period, the waterfall is at its most powerful, creating a mesmerizing sight as the water rushes into the deep gorge.There are a series of waterfalls, some of them 15 to 20 metres.

Venna Lake

Venna Lake is one of the tourist attractions of Mahabaleshwar in Maharashtra state in India. The lake was constructed by Shri Appasaheb Maharaj, who was Raja of Satara in 1842. The lake is surrounded by trees. Tourists can enjoy a boat ride over the lake or a horse ride next to the lake.Tourists can enjoy a boat ride.
